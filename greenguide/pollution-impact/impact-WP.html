water Pollution

Water pollution directly affects critical resources of groundwater and surface water. Both resources are the water supply for our ecosystem, domestic and agricultural industry. Water pollution spreading of infectious diseases through water can affect terrestrial environments, human health and habitation. In some case, economic loss led by water contamination is more directly seen.

Examples of water pollution


High turbidity

The high turbidity of polluted water can provide shelter and foods for the pathogens and microbes. If not treated, the waterborne disease might outbreaks.

Extreme high or low pH

The changing pH made by the pollution can harm the waterborn habitation. For example, water coming out of an coal mine may have a very acidic pH value, which will make the fish dies.

High hardness of water

Many industrial and domestic water users are concerns about the hardness of the water. When the high hardness of water reduce the lifetime and increase the cost of the equipments.

Highly colored water

Highly colored water limited the penetration of lights for those aquatic plants and algal growth. Thus, The highly colored water might have impact on the long term sustainability of aquatic life.
