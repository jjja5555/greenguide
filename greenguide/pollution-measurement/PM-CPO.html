Common Pollutants Overview

water

Common water pollutants include infectious agents (pathogens), organic and inorganic chemicals, plant nutrients, heavy metals and oxygen-demanding wastes, which release from the sources such as sewage, waste, surface runoff and industry.

--Organic pollutants including gasoline, pesticides, fertilizers and cleaning solvent which are commonly used in our daily life. Organic compounds do not dissolve in water, pollution of oil spills will form a separate layer from the water that can be easier visualize and record.

--Heavy metal such as lead, mercury, and arsenic could be contained in the sewage and leak to the ground water or gathered into the surface water. Those metals are extremely toxic with small amount injection, and it is coming from unlined landfills, industrial discharges and mining refuse.

Air

Major air pollution including carbon oxides, nitrogen oxides, sulfur oxides, suspended particulate, volatile organic compounds (VOCs),ozone, radioactive radon (Rn), and toxics.

--Suspended particulate comes from motor vehicles, open fires, soot, tobacco smoke and volcanoes. It includes soot, lead, sulfuric acid, and nitric acid. One of the well-known suspended particulate matter is PM 2.5, which contributes to the smoggy day.

---VOCs includes natural gas methane, isoprene, and benzene, which usually comes out from facilities, aerosol sprays, paint strippers and thinners. Visible Infrared Imaging Radiometer Suite (VIIRS) can be used to tack the natural gas emission.

---Toxics such as chlorine, hydrogen sulfide, formaldehyde, carbon tetrachloride can releasing from building material, dry cleaning, carpets, stuffing and paneling.

Solid Waste

Pollution from waste can be categorized as solid waste and hazardous waste. Some hazardous waste such as battery acid, flea powders, septic tank cleaners, glues and cements contain harmful chemical component. During the landfill, those toxic component can leak into the soil and deposits in the groundwater.
